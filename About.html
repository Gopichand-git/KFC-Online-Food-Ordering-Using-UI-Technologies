About KFC

KFC (Kentucky Fried Chicken) is a global fast-food restaurant chain famous for its secret blend of 11 herbs and spices. Founded by Colonel Harland Sanders in 1952, KFC has become one of the world's most recognizable brands.


Our signature Original Recipe chicken is prepared fresh in-store and cooked to golden perfection. From humble beginnings in Kentucky, we've grown to serve millions of customers worldwide with our delicious chicken and sides.

Our History

1930

Colonel Sanders begins serving chicken at his service station in Kentucky using his secret recipe.

1952

The first KFC franchise opens in Salt Lake City, Utah. The KFC brand officially begins.

1964

Colonel Sanders sells KFC for $2 million but remains as brand ambassador and quality controller.

1987

KFC becomes the first Western fast-food chain to open in China, beginning global expansion.

2020

KFC continues innovation with plant-based options and digital ordering technologies.
